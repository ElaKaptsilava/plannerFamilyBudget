Plan for Your Needs {{ budget.needs_percentage }}%

This section covers your essential expenses. Aim to allocate up to 40% of your after-tax income to these needs:

  • Groceries
  • Housing
  • Basic utilities (electricity, water, gas, internet)
  • Transportation
  • Insurance (health, car, etc.)
  • Minimum debt payments (loans, credit cards) (Anything extra goes towards savings or faster debt repayment)
  • Childcare or other work-related expenses

If your essential expenses exceed {{ needs.total_targets_amount_in_month }}%, you might need to temporarily adjust your "wants" category to make ends meet.

Plan for Your Wants

This section includes discretionary spending on things you enjoy but aren't essential for survival. You allocate around {{ budget.wants_percentage }}% of your income for wants, such as:

  • Dining out
  • Entertainment (movies, hobbies, etc.)
  • Clothing
  • Subscriptions (streaming services, gym memberships)

Sometimes, the line between needs and wants can be blurry. Ask yourself: "Is this essential for my well-being or survival?"

Build in Flexibility (Wiggle Room)

Allocate a small percentage (around 5-10%) for unexpected expenses or occasional treats. This "wiggle room" prevents feeling overly restricted by your budget.

Savings and Debt Repayment {{ budget.savings_percentage }}%

Aim to dedicate {{ budget.savings_percentage }}% or {{ saves }} of your income towards savings and debt repayment. This might include:

  • Emergency fund
  • Retirement savings
  • Debt payments beyond minimums

Remember, this is a guideline. You can adjust the percentages based on your financial situation and goals. Regularly review and update your budget to reflect your changing needs and income.
